Faculty Reflection Guide

AI in Education

Is AI diminishing, leveraging, or genuinely supporting student learning?

This guide offers 27 evidence-grounded questions to help faculty evaluate how AI interacts with learning in their specific courses. It is not a checklist of prohibitions — it is a reflective tool rooted in learning science, designed to help you make intentional, course-specific decisions about when AI supports formation and when it short-circuits it.

The questions are organized across seven dimensions: from alignment and knowledge formation, through scaffolding and critical thinking, to academic integrity and faculty self-reflection. Each question links to the research concept that motivates it.

How to Use
1
Read the question

Each question is always visible. Click it to expand and see the full rationale and research backing.

2
Respond & reflect

Select Yes, No, or Unsure. Add private notes — your responses are saved locally in your browser.

3
Redesign with purpose

Use your notes to inform your syllabus, assignment design, or AI policy — course by course.

4
Start with the diagnostic

Pressed for time? The three-question diagnostic below gives a rapid self-check for any assignment.

Quick Diagnostic — Three Questions for Any Assignment
F

Formation check. Are students still forming the internal standard this assignment is meant to develop — or have they already crossed that threshold?

D

Fading check. Does my course design deliberately remove AI support at the point where independent performance needs to be demonstrated?

T

Transfer check. Will I know — through some other moment in this course — whether the learning happened in the student, or in the tool?
